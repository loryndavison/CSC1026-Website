Internet History

Below is a table showing some of the most significant moments in the evolution of the internet.

Year Event
1930s Vannevar Bush proposes the Memex hypertext device for microfiche
1960s Doug Engelbart and Ted Nelson independently propose "hypertext" ARPANET is born
1970s Ray Tomlinson (BBN) invents email over ARPANET UCL first international connection to ARPANET Cerf and Kahn invent TCP (later split into TCP/IP)
1982 ARPANET adopts TCP/IP - the Internet of TCP/IP internets with 235 hosts
1984 Domain Name System (DNS) introduced JANET connects UK universities to Internet
1988 First Internet worm affects 6,000 of 60,000 hosts
1989 Tim Berners-Lee proposes hypertext system for cern
1990 Tim Berners-Lee writes the world wide web (GUI hypertext browser)
1991 CERN releases world wide web technology (Released to CERN In May and public in August)
1992 26 HTTP servers exist
1993 GUI browsers available for all platforms
1994 W3 consortium (W3C) founded and 1st international WWW conference/ yahoo launch / banner ads appear
1995 World Wide Web takes over FTP as no1 internet service/ Amazon and eBay launch
1996 Browser wars between Netscape and Microsoft begin
1998 Google launches
1998 Napster launches/Bloomberg financial webpage leads to rise of 31% in shares of small technology company
2001 Baltimore train tunnel fire damages backbone fibre optics causing ripple effect across US/ CodeRed worm infiltrates 1000s web servers
2002 Blogging takes off/ DDoS attack knocks 5 of 13 DNS root servers
2003 Nearly half of UK homes connected to Internet/ SQL slammer worm DDoS attack spreads worldwide in 10 minutes
2005 Youtube launches
2007 Google is the most valuable global brand and most visited website

Web Present

The web can currently be said to lie under four main categories:

  • Academic
    • This includes universities and research centres
  • Commercial
    • Business to Consumer
    • Consumer to Consumer
    • Business to Business data exchange and services
  • Social
    • This includes all social media sites, such as Blogs, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, Wikipedia etc.
  • Creative
    • This includes mashups, Open APIs and free access to data.

Web Future

There are different concerns and idea for the future development of the web.

  • Barriers for change
    • The idea of barriers varies across different platforms:
      • For new devices to connect to the web and one another there is virtually no barrier
      • For new applications to run there is a low barrier
      • For new infrastructure changes there is an increasing high barrier
  • Semantic Web
    • This refers to W3's vision of a Web of linked data to enable computers to do more useful work and to develop systems that can support trusted interactions over the network.
  • Cloud Computing
    • The delivery of servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics and more over the Internet
  • Browser/Operating System Blurring
    • The blurring of the lines between online and offline, between the Web and the desktop
    • This allows applications to act more like native applications.
